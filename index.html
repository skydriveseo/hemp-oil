Health Benefits That Can Be Linked with Hemp Oil

Hemp oil is actually extracted from the seeds of the hemp plant. This process is quite similar to extracting olive oil from the seeds of olive using the cold- pressed method. When compared to the cannabis plant that contains almost 28% THC, hemp plants only have just 0.3% of the THC. This is the reason why these products will not cause any ‘high’ effects and are being used for various medicinal purposes.

The hemp oil contains alpha-linolenic acid, gamma-linolenic acid and linoleic acid, a range of three polyunsaturated fatty acids with numerous health benefits. The ratio of linoleic acid to alpha-linolenic acid is 3:1 in hemp oil. And according to experts, humans benefit a lot from this 3:1 fatty acid ratio.

The following are some health benefits that are derived from hemp oil:

Better Skin Texture

Several dermatologists have found that hemp oil can improve symptoms of eczema, or atopic dermatitis. Oil made from hemp seeds has been found to contain fatty acids that are attributed to these results.

Psoriasis, cradle cap, and acne can also be treated with hemp oil. Additionally, hemp oil improves the skin's ability to resist infection.

Better Heart Health

The oil obtained from hemp seeds contains large amounts of linoleic acid. There is strong evidence that consuming a diet high in linoleic acid lowers triglycerides and "disease-causing" cholesterol. Heart disease, stroke, and high blood pressure are all reduced when cholesterol levels are lowered.

Reduces Blood Pressure Levels

People with hypertension may be able to lower their blood pressure with hemp oil's omega-3 fatty acids. To lower blood pressure, supplements full of omega-3 fatty acids are recommended along with blood pressure medication.

Relieves Pain and Reduces Inflammation

In addition to its anti-inflammatory properties, hemp oil also has a calming effect on the body. Hemp oil can relieve pain naturally by being applied directly to the painful area.

There is evidence that hemp oil contains gamma-linoleic acid (GLA) that reduces inflammation. Multiple sclerosis (MS), rheumatoid arthritis (RA), and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) are among the conditions that hemp oil rich in GLA have been found to relieve.

Supporting A Pregnancy in A Healthy Way

Omega-3 fatty acids found in hemp oil make it a valuable supplement for pregnant women. Omega-3 fatty acids are essential during pregnancy, according to studies. Preventing maternal depression may also be possible with this hemp oil. It is also crucial in supporting the baby's healthy brain and eye development.
